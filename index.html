Camera System Preparation

To save you and our technician time on the phone, please follow these steps. This will make your support session as smooth as possible.

1

Locate Serial Number (SN)

Please look on the bottom of your main camera box (the NVR) for a label and find the serial number, often marked as "SN:". This helps us identify your system quickly.

2

Locate Main Hub

Please locate the main box where your camera system is installed (this is often called the NVR or DVR).

3

Find Power Source

See where the camera system's power cord plugs in. Is it a backup battery (UPS), a power strip, or directly into the wall?

4

Power Down

Please unplug the system from its power source (e.g., unplug it from the backup battery, turn off the power strip, or unplug it from the wall).

Urgent Reminder

If there is a white box with 2 cables coming out of it, this must not lose power as this is the Alarm power. This is not to be confused with a white box that has 2 antennas.

5

Wait 60 Seconds

Wait for a full minute to allow the system to shut down completely.

6

Power On

Plug the system back in or turn the power strip back on.

Highly Recommended

While not required, having a monitor/TV (with an HDMI port) and a computer mouse nearby for our call will significantly improve the process. This allows us to see the system's interface directly and guide you more effectively.

Access Control Preparation

To help us identify your device, please have any details you can find ready for our call. Any information you can gather is a great help!

Please look on the device itself (the keypad, card reader, or main control box) for any of the following:

  • Model Number
  • Model Name
  • Serial Number
  • Device Type (e.g., "Keypad," "Card Reader")
  • Brand Name

Don't worry if you can't find all of these. Our technicians will assist you during the call, but having this information ready gives us a valuable head start.

Alarm System Preparation

To help us resolve your alarm issue quickly, please review the section below that best matches your problem. Having this information ready before our call lets us get right to the core of the issue.

General Panel Issues

For problems like a blank screen, connection errors, or general system unresponsiveness, please follow these steps.

1

Go to the Panel

Please go to your main IQ Panel 4 screen.

2

Access Reboot Menu

Swipe down from the top of the screen to open the menu. Tap Settings, then Advanced Settings.

3

Enter Code

You will need your master code to proceed (this is the main 4-digit code used to arm/disarm).

If you do not know your master code, please text us at 832-610-6820 to receive it before our call.

4

Reboot Panel

Tap Reboot Panel and then tap OK to confirm.

5

Wait 3 Minutes

Do not touch the panel. Please wait for a full 3 minutes to allow the system to restart completely. It will show the home screen when it is ready.

Sensor Issues

For problems like "sensor open," "low battery," or "sensor tamper" on a specific door, window, or motion detector, please follow these steps.

1

Locate the Sensor

Please find the exact sensor that is causing the problem (e.g., "Front Door," "Hallway Motion").

2

Inspect the Sensor

  • For Doors/Windows: Look at the sensor and its smaller magnet piece. Are they still aligned? Is the cover on the sensor snapped on tightly?
  • For Motion Detectors: Is anything blocking its view (a new plant, a stack of boxes)?
3

Test the Sensor

Please open and close the door/window that has the problem sensor. Watch the panel screen. Does the panel's status (open/closed) change, or does it stay "open" the whole time?
